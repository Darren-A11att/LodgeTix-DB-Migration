# Attendee/Customer Journey & Jobs-to-be-Done

## Overview

This document maps the complete attendee journey from initial discovery through post-event activities, identifying key jobs-to-be-done, pain points, and opportunities for improvement at each stage.

---

## 1. Discovery & Research Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need to find events that match my interests"
- **Context**: Potential attendees are looking for relevant events among many options
- **Pain Points**:
  - Information overload from multiple event platforms
  - Difficulty filtering by specific interests or criteria
  - Inconsistent event categorization across platforms
  - Missing or vague event descriptions
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement intelligent recommendation algorithms
  - Create detailed filtering options (location, date, type, price range)
  - Develop personalized event discovery based on past attendance
  - Enable saved searches and alerts for new matching events

#### "I need to know if the event is worth attending"
- **Context**: Attendees want to evaluate event quality and value before committing
- **Pain Points**:
  - Lack of authentic reviews or testimonials
  - Limited information about speakers/performers
  - No clear indication of event format or schedule
  - Difficulty assessing networking opportunities
- **Opportunities**:
  - Display verified attendee reviews and ratings
  - Provide detailed speaker/performer profiles
  - Show clear agenda and session descriptions
  - Include past event highlights or recordings

#### "I need to check if my friends are going"
- **Context**: Social proof influences attendance decisions
- **Pain Points**:
  - Privacy concerns about sharing attendance publicly
  - No integration with social networks
  - Can't see mutual connections attending
  - Difficulty coordinating group attendance
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement "Friends Attending" feature with privacy controls
  - Enable group discovery and coordination tools
  - Show anonymized attendance numbers by demographics
  - Create social sharing options with incentives

#### "I need to understand what's included in the ticket"
- **Context**: Clear value proposition is essential for purchase decisions
- **Pain Points**:
  - Unclear or hidden fees
  - Vague descriptions of inclusions
  - No comparison between ticket tiers
  - Missing information about add-ons or extras
- **Opportunities**:
  - Create detailed ticket comparison tables
  - List all inclusions clearly (meals, materials, access levels)
  - Show total price upfront including all fees
  - Provide visual representation of venue access by ticket type

---

## 2. Purchase Decision Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need to compare ticket options and prices"
- **Context**: Attendees want to make informed decisions about ticket purchases
- **Pain Points**:
  - Complex pricing structures
  - Limited-time offers creating pressure
  - Difficulty understanding value differences
  - Hidden costs revealed late in process
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement clear pricing matrix
  - Show savings for early bird or group purchases
  - Provide recommendation engine for best value
  - Display price history and trends

#### "I need to ensure the event fits my schedule"
- **Context**: Time commitment and conflicts are major considerations
- **Pain Points**:
  - Unclear event duration or schedule
  - No calendar integration options
  - Time zone confusion for virtual/hybrid events
  - Can't see potential conflicts with other commitments
- **Opportunities**:
  - Enable calendar integration (Google, Outlook, Apple)
  - Show clear start/end times in user's time zone
  - Provide schedule breakdown for multi-day events
  - Offer partial attendance options where applicable

#### "I need to know the refund policy"
- **Context**: Risk mitigation is important for commitment
- **Pain Points**:
  - Buried or unclear refund terms
  - No option for ticket transfer
  - Strict no-refund policies
  - Complex refund request process
- **Opportunities**:
  - Display refund policy prominently
  - Offer flexible ticket transfer options
  - Implement tiered refund schedules
  - Provide ticket insurance options

#### "I need to coordinate with my group"
- **Context**: Many attend events as part of a group
- **Pain Points**:
  - Difficult to purchase multiple tickets with individual details
  - No group communication tools
  - Can't split payments easily
  - Challenging to manage changes for group bookings
- **Opportunities**:
  - Create group booking workflows
  - Enable payment splitting options
  - Provide group coordination tools
  - Allow group organizer to manage attendee details

---

## 3. Registration & Purchase Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need a quick and easy checkout process"
- **Context**: Friction in checkout leads to abandonment
- **Pain Points**:
  - Too many form fields required
  - No guest checkout option
  - Slow page load times
  - Required account creation
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement one-click checkout for returning users
  - Minimize required fields
  - Enable guest checkout
  - Optimize for mobile experience

#### "I need to register multiple attendees"
- **Context**: Often purchasing for others (family, team, friends)
- **Pain Points**:
  - Repetitive data entry for each attendee
  - Can't save attendee information for future use
  - Difficulty managing different ticket types
  - No bulk upload option for large groups
- **Opportunities**:
  - Create attendee profile saving
  - Enable CSV upload for bulk registration
  - Implement copy attendee details function
  - Provide group registration templates

#### "I need to pay using my preferred method"
- **Context**: Payment flexibility increases conversion
- **Pain Points**:
  - Limited payment options
  - No digital wallet support
  - Currency conversion issues
  - Corporate payment challenges
- **Opportunities**:
  - Support multiple payment methods (cards, digital wallets, bank transfers)
  - Enable installment payment plans
  - Provide invoice options for corporate buyers
  - Support multiple currencies

#### "I need confirmation of my purchase"
- **Context**: Immediate confirmation provides peace of mind
- **Pain Points**:
  - Delayed confirmation emails
  - Unclear next steps
  - No instant ticket access
  - Confirmation lost in spam
- **Opportunities**:
  - Send instant confirmation with tickets
  - Provide clear post-purchase instructions
  - Enable immediate calendar saves
  - Implement SMS confirmation option

---

## 4. Pre-Event Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need to access my tickets easily"
- **Context**: Attendees need reliable ticket access
- **Pain Points**:
  - Tickets buried in email
  - No mobile wallet integration
  - PDF tickets difficult on mobile
  - Can't find tickets when needed
- **Opportunities**:
  - Integrate with Apple/Google Wallet
  - Create dedicated mobile app
  - Provide multiple access methods
  - Enable offline ticket access

#### "I need to get event updates and reminders"
- **Context**: Staying informed enhances experience
- **Pain Points**:
  - Information overload from too many emails
  - Missing important updates
  - No customization of communication preferences
  - Updates not reaching attendees
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement smart notification system
  - Allow communication preference settings
  - Use multiple channels (email, SMS, app)
  - Create countdown and reminder sequences

#### "I need to plan my travel and accommodation"
- **Context**: Logistics planning is crucial for attendance
- **Pain Points**:
  - No venue/location details provided
  - Missing transportation options
  - No accommodation recommendations
  - Lack of local area information
- **Opportunities**:
  - Provide comprehensive venue guides
  - Partner with travel/accommodation providers
  - Offer group accommodation blocks
  - Create local area guides

#### "I need to connect with other attendees"
- **Context**: Networking starts before the event
- **Pain Points**:
  - No attendee directory or networking tools
  - Privacy concerns about sharing information
  - Can't identify relevant connections
  - No pre-event engagement opportunities
- **Opportunities**:
  - Build opt-in attendee networking platform
  - Enable interest-based matching
  - Create pre-event discussion forums
  - Facilitate meet-up planning

---

## 5. Event Day Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need to check in quickly"
- **Context**: Smooth entry sets positive tone
- **Pain Points**:
  - Long check-in queues
  - Technical issues with digital tickets
  - Name not found on list
  - Multiple check-in points confusion
- **Opportunities**:
  - Implement QR code fast-track check-in
  - Enable pre-event check-in
  - Provide self-service kiosks
  - Create express lanes for digital tickets

#### "I need to navigate the venue"
- **Context**: Large or unfamiliar venues can be challenging
- **Pain Points**:
  - Poor signage or wayfinding
  - No digital maps available
  - Can't find specific sessions/areas
  - Accessibility concerns
- **Opportunities**:
  - Provide interactive venue maps
  - Implement indoor navigation
  - Show real-time session locations
  - Highlight accessibility routes

#### "I need to access my ticket on my phone"
- **Context**: Mobile access is increasingly expected
- **Pain Points**:
  - Poor internet connectivity at venue
  - Battery drain concerns
  - Screen brightness issues for scanning
  - App crashes or glitches
- **Opportunities**:
  - Ensure offline ticket functionality
  - Optimize for low battery mode
  - Provide backup access methods
  - Enable screenshot-proof tickets

#### "I need help if issues arise"
- **Context**: Problems need quick resolution
- **Pain Points**:
  - Can't find help desk or staff
  - Long wait times for assistance
  - Staff lack information or authority
  - No self-service options
- **Opportunities**:
  - Deploy roaming help staff
  - Create in-app support chat
  - Provide comprehensive FAQ
  - Enable peer-to-peer help system

---

## 6. Post-Event Phase

### Jobs-to-be-Done

#### "I need to provide feedback"
- **Context**: Attendees want their voice heard
- **Pain Points**:
  - Survey fatigue from long forms
  - No incentive to provide feedback
  - Can't see if feedback makes a difference
  - Generic questions not relevant
- **Opportunities**:
  - Create short, engaging feedback forms
  - Offer incentives for completion
  - Show how feedback is implemented
  - Ask specific, actionable questions

#### "I need to access event content/recordings"
- **Context**: Maximize value from event attendance
- **Pain Points**:
  - No recordings available
  - Limited time access to content
  - Poor quality recordings
  - Can't find specific sessions
- **Opportunities**:
  - Provide automatic recording access
  - Create searchable content library
  - Offer presentation downloads
  - Enable content sharing permissions

#### "I need to connect with people I met"
- **Context**: Networking value extends beyond event
- **Pain Points**:
  - Lost business cards
  - No digital connection platform
  - Can't remember all connections
  - No follow-up facilitation
- **Opportunities**:
  - Digital business card exchange
  - AI-powered connection recommendations
  - Automated follow-up reminders
  - Integration with professional networks

#### "I need receipts for expense reports"
- **Context**: Professional attendees need documentation
- **Pain Points**:
  - Receipts not detailed enough
  - Can't get receipts after the fact
  - Multiple receipts for group bookings
  - Wrong format for company requirements
- **Opportunities**:
  - Provide detailed, customizable receipts
  - Enable receipt re-sending anytime
  - Support multiple receipt formats
  - Include all necessary tax information

## Key Insights & Recommendations

### Cross-Journey Themes

1. **Mobile-First Experience**: Every touchpoint should be optimized for mobile devices
2. **Personalization**: Use data to customize experiences throughout the journey
3. **Transparency**: Clear information about pricing, policies, and expectations
4. **Social Integration**: Enable connection and coordination at every stage
5. **Flexibility**: Provide options for different attendee needs and preferences

### Priority Improvements

1. **High Impact, Low Effort**:
   - Clear refund policy display
   - Mobile wallet integration
   - Automated reminders
   - Digital receipt management

2. **High Impact, Medium Effort**:
   - Group booking tools
   - Pre-event networking platform
   - Multi-channel communication
   - Quick check-in systems

3. **Transformational Opportunities**:
   - AI-powered event recommendations
   - Integrated travel planning
   - Comprehensive networking ecosystem
   - Predictive attendee services

### Success Metrics

- **Discovery**: Click-through rates, search-to-purchase conversion
- **Purchase**: Cart abandonment rates, average transaction time
- **Pre-Event**: Ticket access frequency, update engagement rates
- **Event Day**: Check-in times, support ticket volume
- **Post-Event**: Feedback completion rates, content engagement

## Conclusion

Understanding and optimizing the attendee journey requires continuous iteration based on user feedback and behavior data. By addressing the identified pain points and capitalizing on opportunities at each stage, event platforms can significantly improve attendee satisfaction, increase conversion rates, and build lasting relationships with their users.
